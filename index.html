Insurance Claims Process

1

Report the Accident

Contact your insurance company immediately after the accident. Provide all necessary details including time, location, and involved parties.

2

Document Everything

Take photos of damage, gather witness information, and keep all receipts related to the accident and temporary transportation.

3

Vehicle Inspection

An adjuster will inspect your vehicle to assess damage and determine if it's repairable or a total loss.

4

Receive Settlement Offer

Review the settlement offer carefully. You have the right to negotiate if you believe the valuation is too low.

5

Finalize the Claim

Once you accept the settlement, transfer the vehicle title and receive your payment. Handle any outstanding loan balance.

Key Regulatory Points

Consumer Rights

You have the right to dispute valuations, request independent appraisals, and appeal insurance decisions in most states.

Salvage Titles

Total loss vehicles typically receive salvage titles, which affect future resale value and insurance coverage options.

Time Limits

Most states require insurance companies to settle total loss claims within 30-60 days of receiving all necessary documentation.
